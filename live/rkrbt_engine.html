// VIRTUAL CABLE SETUP REQUIRED FOR BROWSER AUDIO

STEP 1: ROUTE CHROME (SUNO) AUDIO TO VIRTUAL CABLE

  • Open Windows Settings > System > Sound > Volume mixer (or search "Sound Mixer Options").
  • Find the app playing your music (e.g., Google Chrome, Edge).
  • Change its Output dropdown from "Default" to CABLE Input (VB-Audio Virtual Cable).
  • (Result: The visualizer bars will now start jumping to the music, but your earphones will go silent).

STEP 2: LISTEN TO THE CABLE IN YOUR EARPHONES

  • Press Windows Key + R, type mmsys.cpl, and press Enter.
  • Click the Recording tab at the top.
  • Right-click CABLE Output (or VB-Audio Virtual Cable) and select Properties.
  • Click the Listen tab and check the box "Listen to this device".
  • In the dropdown right below it ("Playback through this device"), select your personal Speakers / Earphones.
  • Click Apply and OK.

STEP 3: ENGINE CONFIGURATION

  • Ensure the engine's AUDIO SRC (top right) is set to [VB] to force the visualizer to lock onto the Virtual Cable.
  • Use the GAIN (- / +) controls slowly on the main console to adjust visualizer sensitivity.
